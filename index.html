More About
There are a lot of applications of speech in the Artificial Intelligence (AI) domain. The most popular applications are speech recognition, speaker verification, speech synthesis, emotion recognition, spoof detection, language identification, speech watermarking, and disease prediction. The first and popular application of speech is speech recognition, also known as automatic speech recognition (ASR), computer speech recognition, or speech-to-text, which is a capability that enables a program to process human speech into a written format. Applications of ASR are virtual assistants, online banking, E-commerce, health care system, etc. The second most popular application is speaker verification where the objective is the authentication of a claimed identity from measurements on the voice signal. Applications of speaker verification include entry control to restricted premises, access to privileged information, funds transfer, credit card authorization, voice banking, and similar transactions. Another popular application of speech is speech synthesis. Speech synthesis is the artificial production of human speech. A computer system used for this purpose is called a speech computer or speech synthesizer and can be implemented in software or hardware products. A text-to-speech (TTS) system converts normal language text into speech. Applications of speech synthesis are voice output communication aids, entertainment productions such as games and animations, E-learning, etc. The next popular application of speech is speech emotion recognition, abbreviated as SER, which is the act of attempting to recognize human emotion and affective states from speech. This is capitalizing on the fact that voice often reflects underlying emotion through tone and pitch. Applications of SER in a multitude of real-life contexts, e.g., human-computer interaction systems such as interactive movies, storytelling, and E-tutoring applications, retrieval and indexing of the video/audio files, improving interaction at the call center, assisting psychological treatments, surveillance systems, etc. After completing this course participants will be able to develop speech-based AI applications as well as new research insights in this domain.
